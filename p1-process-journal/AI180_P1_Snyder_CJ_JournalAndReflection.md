# Bias Artifact
## CJ Snyder | Game Design | AI 180
The four bias cards each document one cognitive pattern found in your session:
Einstellung Effect is the first and arguably the root bias. Before seeing what the AI could offer, you told it not to deviate from your existing coding style. That constraint came from prior experience — your familiarity with your own approach literally blocked you from evaluating alternatives. The card quotes the moment directly: "Tell AI: don't change my style."
Confirmation Bias follows from there. The AI returned two different fixes, and you accepted both without questioning which was better or whether either was correct. This is the bias in its clearest form — you wanted the problem solved, the AI said it was solved, and that was enough. The critical step of evaluating the output against an independent standard got skipped.
Automation Bias is the one your chart implied but didn't name. After applying the fixes, you asked the AI to scan for any remaining issues. That sounds like due diligence, but it's actually delegating your verification to the same system that generated the problem. The AI can't catch errors introduced by its own blind spots.
Anchoring is the foundational condition underneath all three. Your chart begins with "open AI tool without deciding what I want it to do." When you have no prior intention, the AI's first output becomes the reference point for everything that follows. You end up measuring the work against itself rather than against a goal you defined.
The counter tips at the bottom of each card connect directly to the ESF seed practice from your brief — specifically the two questions: "What am I testing?" and "What will I not let the AI decide for me?" Each counter is a version of those questions applied to that specific bias moment.
The artifact is designed so the form performs the bias, not just labels it. The workflow strip makes you trace the sequence before expanding any card, which mirrors how the biases actually operated — sequentially, each one enabled by the last.
